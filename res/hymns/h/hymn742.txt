["1 Man is a being of three parts\nThe body, soul, and spirit too\nTh' eternal purpose to fulfill,\nWith God's inheritance in view.", "2 The body is the outward part,\nThat man a world-sense may possess;\nThus he may touch the outward things,\nAnd bodily himself express.", "3 The soul within-the inward part-\nIs but the very self of man;\nThe sense of self it gives to him,\nTo touch the psychic world of man.", "4 The spirit is the inmost part,\nWith which the Lord he may receive;\nThe sense of God it gives to man,\nThat he the spirit-world perceive.", "5 Within the soul of man there are,\nThe mind, emotion, and the will;\nThese are the functions for the man,\nHis human nature to fulfill.", "6 Within the spirit of the man,\nAre found the conscience, fellowship\nAnd intuition, which thereby,\nThe man to worship God equip.", "7 The man must exercise his soul,\nTo choose that God he may possess\nAnd by his spirit God partake\nAnd thru his body God express.", "8 Man's spirit has to be reborn,\nWith life divine to him conveyed;\nHis soul must be transformed by God\nHis body like Christ's body made.", "9 'Tis by these steps of work divine,\nIn each of man's three separate parts,\nThat man is mingled with the Lord,\nAnd His expression full imparts."]
